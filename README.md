# Provenance Guard

Provenance Guard estimates whether a piece of text-based content (a poem, a short
story excerpt, a blog post) was **written by a human or generated by an AI**. It
returns an attribution result, a confidence score, and a plain-language
**transparency label** for the reader. Creators can **appeal** a classification,
and every decision and appeal is recorded in a structured **audit log**.

Architecture, the detection-signal rationale, and the request flow live in
[planning.md](./planning.md). This README is the canonical record of the
graded evidence: the label variants, rate-limit config, appeal handling, and audit
log.

## Transparency label — the three variants (verbatim text)

The label communicates the attribution result in plain language and makes the
confidence level meaningful to a non-technical reader. `{pct}` is replaced with the
confidence percentage in the chosen verdict (e.g. `92%`).

| Variant | Trigger | Exact text displayed |
|---------|---------|----------------------|
| **High-confidence AI** | verdict `ai`, signals agree, confidence high | `🤖 Likely AI-Generated — Our analysis indicates this content was most likely produced by an AI system (confidence: {pct}%). Two independent signals — writing-structure analysis and a language-model review — agreed on this assessment. Attribution is an estimate, not proof. If you created this yourself, you can appeal this label.` |
| **High-confidence human** | verdict `human`, signals agree, confidence high | `✍️ Likely Human-Written — Our analysis indicates this content was most likely written by a person (confidence: {pct}%). Two independent signals agreed on this assessment. Attribution is an estimate, not a certainty — if you disagree, you can appeal this label.` |
| **Uncertain** | verdict `uncertain` (score near the middle, or signals disagree) | `❓ Uncertain Attribution — We could not confidently determine whether this content was written by a human or by an AI (confidence: {pct}%). Our two signals were inconclusive or disagreed with each other. Please treat this result with caution — it should not be used as proof of authorship. You can appeal this label.` |

Quoted (for unambiguous copy):

## Detection signals (summary)

Two distinct signals that fail in different ways (full rationale in
[planning.md](./planning.md#detection-signals)):

1. **Structural statistics (burstiness)** — deterministic, local. Measures sentence-
   length variance and repetition. Humans write bursty; AI writes uniform. Blind to
   short texts, naturally-uniform human prose, and meaning.
2. **LLM-as-judge (Groq)** — measures stylistic/semantic fingerprints of model
   output. Non-deterministic, vulnerable to prompt-injection and ESL bias.

When they **agree**, confidence is high; when they **disagree**, confidence drops and
the reader sees the *uncertain* label.

## Confidence scoring

Confidence reflects both how far the fused score is from the undecided middle (0.5)
and how much the two signals agreed. A 0.51 case yields the *uncertain* label; a 0.95
case yields a high-confidence label. _Testing approach + results: to be filled in
during implementation._

## API

- `POST /submit` — submit text; returns attribution result, confidence, label text,
  `content_id`, and per-signal breakdown.
- `POST /appeal/<content_id>` — contest a classification; captures reasoning, logs it
  beside the original decision, sets status to `under review`.
- `GET /log` — structured audit log of all decisions and appeals.

## Appeals workflow

An appeal captures the creator's reasoning, logs it alongside the original decision,
and updates the content's status to `under review`. Automated re-classification is
not performed — a human reviews. _Worked example: to be filled in during
implementation._

---

## Audit log

Every attribution decision records: `id`, timestamp, text excerpt, both signal
sub-scores, fused score, confidence, verdict, label shown, signals used, and status;
appeals are appended beside the original decision. _Sample with ≥3 entries (or
`GET /log` output): to be filled in during implementation._
